## 2 Thessalonians 1:3 (id: 167075)

**For what two things in the church at Thessalonica does Paul give thanks to God?**

Paul give thanks to God for their growing faith and for their love toward each other.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:3

## 2 Thessalonians 1:4 (id: 167076)

**What circumstances are the believers enduring in Thessalonica?**

The believers are enduring persecutions and afflictions.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:4

## 2 Thessalonians 1:5 (id: 167077)

**What will be the positive result of the circumstances the believers are enduring?**

The believers will be counted worthy of the kingdom of God.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:5

## 2 Thessalonians 1:6–8 (id: 167078)

**What will God do to those who afflict the believers?**

God will afflict those who afflict the believers, punishing them with flaming fire.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:6–2TH 1:8

## 2 Thessalonians 1:7 (id: 167079)

**When will the believers be relieved of their afflictions?**

The believers will be relieved when Jesus Christ is revealed from heaven.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:7

## 2 Thessalonians 1:9 (id: 167080)

**How long will the punishment be for those who do not know God?**

The punishment for those who do not know God will be eternal.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:9

## 2 Thessalonians 1:9 (#2) (id: 167081)

**From what are those who do not know God separated as part of their punishment?**

Those who do not know God are separated from the presence of the Lord as part of their punishment.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:9

## 2 Thessalonians 1:10 (id: 167082)

**What will the believers do when they see Christ come on his day?**

The believers will marvel at Christ when he comes on his day.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:10

## 2 Thessalonians 1:11–12 (id: 167083)

**What is the result of the believers’ good works of faith done in God’s power?**

The result of their good works is that the name of the Lord Jesus Christ is glorified.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 1:11–2TH 1:12

## 2 Thessalonians 2:1 (id: 167084)

**About what event does Paul say he is now going to write?**

Paul says he is now going to write concerning the coming of the Lord Jesus Christ.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:1

## 2 Thessalonians 2:2 (id: 167085)

**What does Paul tell them not to believe?**

Paul tells them not to believe that the day of the Lord has already come.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:2

## 2 Thessalonians 2:3 (id: 167086)

**What does Paul say must come before the day of the Lord?**

The falling away and the revealing of the man of lawlessness must come before the day of the Lord.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:3

## 2 Thessalonians 2:4 (id: 167087)

**What does the man of lawlessness do?**

The man of lawlessness opposes and exalts himself against God, sitting in the temple of God and portraying himself as God.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:4

## 2 Thessalonians 2:6–7 (id: 167088)

**When will the man of lawlessness be revealed?**

The man of lawlessness will be revealed when it is time, when the one who restrains him is taken out of the way.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:6–2TH 2:7

## 2 Thessalonians 2:8 (id: 167089)

**What will Jesus do to the man of lawlessness when Jesus is revealed?**

When Jesus is revealed, he will kill the man of lawlessness.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:8

## 2 Thessalonians 2:9 (id: 167090)

**Who is working with the man of lawlessness to give him power, signs, and false wonders?**

Satan is working with the man of lawlessness to give him power, signs, and false wonders.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:9

## 2 Thessalonians 2:10 (id: 167091)

**Why are some deceived by the man of lawlessness and are perishing?**

Some are deceived because they did not receive the love of the truth, that they might be saved.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:10

## 2 Thessalonians 2:12 (id: 167092)

**In what do those who are deceived and perishing take pleasure?**

Those who are deceived and perishing take pleasure in unrighteousness.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:12

## 2 Thessalonians 2:13–14 (id: 167093)

**What did God choose for the Thessalonians to obtain through the gospel?**

God chose for the Thessalonians to obtain the glory of the Lord Jesus Christ through the gospel.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:13–2TH 2:14

## 2 Thessalonians 2:15 (id: 167094)

**What does Paul call the Thessalonians to do now that they have received the gospel?**

Paul calls the Thessalonians to stand firm and to grasp the traditions that they were taught.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:15

## 2 Thessalonians 2:17 (id: 167095)

**In what does Paul desire the Thessalonians to be established in their hearts?**

Paul desires that the Thessalonians be established in every good work and word.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 2:17

## 2 Thessalonians 3:1 (id: 167096)

**For what does Paul want the Thessalonians to pray regarding the word of the Lord?**

Paul wants the Thessalonians to pray that the word of the Lord would spread rapidly and be glorified.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:1

## 2 Thessalonians 3:2 (id: 167097)

**From whom does Paul wish to be delivered?**

Paul wishes to be delivered from wicked and evil people who do not have faith.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:2

## 2 Thessalonians 3:4 (id: 167098)

**What does Paul tell the Thessalonians to continue to do?**

Paul tells the Thessalonians to continue to do the things he has commanded them.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:4

## 2 Thessalonians 3:6 (id: 167099)

**How are the believers to react to every brother who walks disorderly and not according to the traditions they received from Paul?**

The believers are to avoid every brother who walks disorderly and not according to the traditions they received from Paul.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:6

## 2 Thessalonians 3:7–8 (id: 167100)

**What example did Paul set for the Thessalonians regarding his work and support?**

Paul labored night and day, paying for his food, and not being a burden to anyone.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:7–2TH 3:8

## 2 Thessalonians 3:10 (id: 167101)

**What did Paul command concerning anyone who does not want to work?**

Paul commanded that anyone who does not want to work should not eat.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:10

## 2 Thessalonians 3:12 (id: 167102)

**Instead of being lazy, what does Paul command such people to do?**

Paul commands the lazy to work with quietness and to eat their own food.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:12

## 2 Thessalonians 3:14 (id: 167103)

**What are the brothers to do with anyone who does not obey Paul’s instruction in this letter?**

The brothers are to have no association with anyone who does not obey Paul’s instruction in this letter.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:14

## 2 Thessalonians 3:16 (id: 167104)

**What does Paul desire that the Lord would give the Thessalonians?**

Paul desires that the Lord would give the Thessalonians peace at all times in all ways.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:16

## 2 Thessalonians 3:17 (id: 167105)

**How does Paul show that he is the author of this letter?**

Paul wrote the greeting with his own hand as the sign that he is the author.

* **Associated Passages:** 2TH 3:17
